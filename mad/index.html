Food on the trail

Food gives a walking day meaning. Simple recipes for the campfire, the trangia and the kitchen at home, so you eat well both on the trail and when you dream your way back.

Guides in food

Food

Banana Pancakes for Outdoor Adventures

Delicious banana pancakes made with banana, egg and oats. Easy energy food you can fry over a fire or on a Trangia and take along the way.

Food

Campfire Goulash for Four

Heartwarming goulash with beef, paprika and root vegetables. It simmers tender over a fire or Trangia and fills you up after a long day on the trail.

Food

Chili con Carne for the Campfire

Rich chili con carne with beef, beans, corn and spices. A filling pot with warmth and bite, perfect for an evening around the fire.

Food

Homemade Pizza for Outdoor Adventures

Pizza from scratch with a crisp base, homemade tomato sauce and fresh toppings. Bake it in the oven or over the fire after a day in the open.

Food

Pickled Red Onions for Trail Lunches

Crisp, tangy and sweet pickled red onions that lift any dish. Easy to make at home and bring along as a colourful kick for your trail food.

Food

Homemade Pizza Dough from Scratch

A soft, airy pizza dough with only a handful of ingredients. Knead it, let it rise, and you have the base for a genuinely good pizza, at home or on the trail.

Food

Swedish Sausage Stew over the Fire

Korv stroganoff, the Swedish classic with sausages in a creamy paprika sauce. Rich and easy comfort food that also works on a camping stove.

Food

Boiled Chestnuts on the Camino

Healthy, tasty chestnuts cooked on the trail. All you need is chestnuts, water, salt and a pot. A cheap meal and a fine snack for the trip.
