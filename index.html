The ways to Santiago

Choose your Camino

Every route ends at the same cathedral. They just ask different things of you on the way. Distances are the classic full routes, and you can always walk a shorter stretch.

Camino Francés

The classic

from Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port

The classic route, and the one most people mean when they say the Camino.

771 km 31 to 35 days

Camino Portugués

from Lisbon or Porto

Gentler climbs, coastal variants and a milder introduction to the Camino.

616 km 25 to 28 days from Lisbon

Camino del Norte

from Irún

The coastal route along the Bay of Biscay. Quieter, harder, greener.

824 km 32 to 36 days

Camino Primitivo

from Oviedo

The original route. Mountainous, wild and deeply rewarding.

313 km 12 to 14 days

Camino Inglés

from Ferrol

The short one. A whole Camino feeling in a single week.

116 km 5 to 6 days

Vía de la Plata

from Seville

The long, lonely trek from south to north for experienced walkers.

960 km 38 to 42 days

Camino Finisterre

from Santiago de Compostela

The continuation to the end of the world, where pilgrims of old walked out to the sea.

89 km 3 to 4 days

About

The way is better when you share it

Camino King started as travel notes from my first Camino in 2016 and grew into the place I wish I had found before my own first step: clear routes, honest gear and stories that get you out the door.

It was never only about the kilometres. It is about the people you meet along the way and the feeling of standing on a ridge with the whole day laid out in front of you. Everything here has been walked, worn or weighed before it gets recommended.
