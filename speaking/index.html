Speaking

Want us to talk about the Camino?

An open invitation from someone who has walked the routes. Not a speaker with a show, just a hiker sharing what the way has taught him.

Camino King lives mostly online, but the Camino deserves to be told in a room full of real people too. If you run a club, a workplace or a hiking group where someone is quietly dreaming about setting off, we would love to come by and tell you what it is really like.

Who is behind it

Behind Camino King is Michael Kirkegaard, who walked his first Camino in 2016 and never quite came all the way home again. Since then it has become the Camino Francés twice, the way out to Finisterre four times and the Camino Portugués, plus a long list of smaller caminos in Denmark and other parts of Europe.

That is the experience you get. Not a polished script, but honest stories from someone who has packed the backpack wrong, blistered, lost the way and found it again. That is often what people remember afterwards, and it is what makes someone brave enough to take their own first step.

What we can talk about

We always shape the content around you and your audience. Here are some of the threads an evening can follow.

Walking the Camino

What it is like to stand there with your backpack on the first morning, how the days fall into rhythm, and what actually happens to you along the way. The big moments and the small ones no one tells you about in advance.

Preparation and training

How you walk yourself ready from home, how much time you really need, and what your body has to get used to before you reach the starting line in St. Jean or Sarria.

Packing and gear

What to bring, what can stay home, and why the backpack should weigh less than most people think. Honest advice on shoes, base weight and the mistakes you only make once.

The routes

The difference between the Camino Francés, the Camino Portugués and the way onward to Finisterre. Where they bite, where they open up, and how you choose the one that fits you.

The quiet of the trail

Why so many people come home changed, what the rhythm of walking does to your head, and how a week on the trail sometimes moves you more than a whole year at home.

The practical side

Hostels, stamps and the pilgrim passport, food for the long days, budget and the questions everyone asks before they set off. Answers from someone who has stood in it himself.

Who is it for

Clubs, companies, hiking groups and evening schools. Anywhere people gather and someone is carrying the Camino in the back of their mind. It suits both those weighing up their first trip and those who simply love a good story from the trail.

Book quietly, have a chat

There is no price list and no fixed package. Just an open invitation. Write a few words about you, what brings you together and what you would like to hear, and we will figure out the rest together.

Write to mail@michaelkirkegaard.dk We reply to every message, the curious ones included.

Frequently asked questions about the talk

Have you given a Camino talk before?
Honest answer: no, not yet. This is an open invitation, not a track record. What I do have is the routes, walked many times, the first back in 2016, and I gladly share what the way has taught me. You get someone who has stood in it, not someone reading from a brochure.
What can you talk about?
Walking the Camino, preparation and training, packing and gear, the different routes, the quiet of the trail and all the practical parts with hostels, stamps and food. We shape the content around you so it lands where your audience is curious. If you want a taste, read along in our Camino glossary and guides.
Who is it for?
Clubs, companies, hiking groups, evening schools and anywhere else that gathers people who dream about the Camino. It works both for those weighing up their first trip and for those who simply love a good story from the trail.
Where and when can it happen?
We figure that out together. A cosy evening in the clubhouse, a slot at the workplace or something else entirely. Write with what you have in mind and we will see if it can work.
What does it cost?
There is no price list. It depends on the setting, the length and the travel. Send an email with a few words about you and we will have an open chat about what makes sense.

Curious about the Camino already?

While we find a date, you can wander through the universe. The routes, the words and all the practical parts are ready here.
