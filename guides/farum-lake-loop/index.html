Farum Lake Loop: the Best 10 km Route on Zealand?

It is about 10 km around Farum Lake. See the map, facts, parking and sights like Gretesholm on one of north Zealand's best walking routes.

Are you looking for a beautiful 10 kilometre route on Zealand? Then Farum Lake is definitely worth a visit. We are well on our way to mapping all the best walking routes in the Danish kingdom, and there is soon not a lake on Zealand we have not been around.

If you need a map and a bit of extra info, read on. Our route quickly turned into 14 kilometres because of a small extra detour and a bit of trouble with the GPS signal.

Farum Lake map

Farum Lake is a beautiful lake located in Farum in north Zealand. We have a dedicated walking club called Caminoking, where we share our route around the lake. If you want to follow our walking route and see details of our trips, you will find us on Strava under the name Caminoking. Come and experience the scenic route and join the walking club at Farum Lake.

How far is it around Farum Lake?

Farum Lake is a scenic lake that draws both local residents and visitors from further afield. One of the most common questions is how far it is around.

The route around Farum Lake stretches over about 10 kilometres. The distance includes the entire perimeter of the lake and offers a lovely walk or run along the water’s edge. The exact distance can vary a little depending on the route you choose and any detours along the way.

A popular route is to follow the path that runs along the lakeshore. The well-maintained path gives a pleasant and easy walk with views over the lake’s beautiful water and the surrounding landscape. The Farum Lake area also has other paths and walking trails that lead into the surrounding forests and nature areas, so you can add variety to your trip. Depending on the route, the total distance can be a little longer or shorter than the 10 kilometres mentioned.

Whether you are a keen walker, a runner or simply want a relaxing stroll, a trip around Farum Lake is a wonderful way to immerse yourself in nature. So put on your hiking socks, prepare for a beautiful nature experience and explore this natural gem.

More facts about Farum Lake

Here are some quick facts about Farum Lake:

  • Location: Farum Lake lies in northern Zealand and is surrounded by the towns of Farum, Værløse and Birkerød. It is part of the area’s scenic landscape.
  • Size: The lake covers an area of around 2.3 square kilometres, making it a relatively large lake in the region.
  • Depth: The maximum depth is around 8 metres. It varies, though, with the water level and the season.
  • Scenic surroundings: The lake is surrounded by lush forests, open meadows and colourful flowers that create a picturesque setting.
  • Wildlife: The lake is home to rich wildlife. You can spot bird species such as ducks, geese, swans and wading birds, as well as smaller animals like squirrels and hares in the surrounding forests.
  • Recreational options: Besides walking, the lake is a popular spot for cycling, running and birdwatching, and there are well-laid-out paths and routes.
  • Cultural sights: The area offers historic sites such as the manor house Gretesholm and the burial mound Gretteshøj, which give an insight into the region’s history.

Gretesholm and Gretteshøj

Gretesholm and Gretteshøj are two historic sights near Farum Lake. Here is a little about each of them.

Gretesholm

Gretesholm is an old manor house dating back to the 1700s. The manor was originally built as a hunting lodge and later expanded into a larger building. Gretesholm is surrounded by beautiful parkland and a well-kept garden that create an idyllic atmosphere. The manor has a rich history and has been owned by several prominent families over time. The impressive architecture and the historic setting make it an interesting place to visit for those with an interest in Denmark’s cultural heritage.

Gretteshøj

Gretteshøj is an ancient burial mound located near Gretesholm. It is one of the largest burial mounds in the area and dates back to the late Stone Age or the Bronze Age. The mound is shaped like a conical hill with a diameter of around 40 metres and a height of around 4 metres. Gretteshøj is an important archaeological monument and bears witness to the burial rituals of earlier times.

Both Gretesholm and Gretteshøj add historical and cultural depth to the area around Farum Lake and are worth exploring if you want to learn more about Denmark’s past.

Farum Lake parking

There are several parking options at Farum Lake:

  • Car park at Farum Lake: There is a dedicated car park near the lake. It is usually the most convenient and closest option for those who want to explore the lake area. Be aware that there may be restrictions or parking charges at certain times.
  • Surrounding streets: If the car park is full, you can find parking on the surrounding streets. Be aware of any parking restrictions and follow the local rules to avoid fines.
  • Alternative transport: If you want to avoid parking hassle, you can take public transport close to the lake. Check the timetables and options in your area so you can plan the journey.

When planning your trip to Farum Lake, we always recommend double-checking parking options and rules. That way you ensure a smooth and pleasant experience.

Frequently asked questions

How far is it around Farum Lake?
It is about 10 kilometres around Farum Lake if you follow the paths along the shore. If you take detours into the surrounding forests, the trip becomes longer. Our own route quickly turned into 14 kilometres because of a small detour and a bit of trouble with the GPS signal.
Is the route around Farum Lake hard to walk?
No. The terrain is fairly easy, and a large part of the route follows a well-maintained path along the shore. The route is therefore accessible to most people regardless of experience and fitness.
Where can you park at Farum Lake?
There is a dedicated car park near the lake, which is often the easiest option. If it is full, you can find parking on the surrounding streets. Be aware of local parking rules and any charges.
Can you take public transport to Farum Lake?
Yes. If you want to avoid parking hassle, you can take a bus or train close to the lake. Check the timetables in your area so you can plan the trip in good time.
What sights are there at Farum Lake?
By the lake you will find, among other things, Gretesholm, an old manor house from the 1700s, and Gretteshøj, a large ancient burial mound about 40 metres in diameter. The area is also home to rich bird and animal life.
What can you do at Farum Lake besides walking?
Farum Lake is popular for running, cycling and birdwatching. There are well-laid-out paths, picnic areas and benches, and the surrounding forests and nature areas give plenty of scope to vary the trip.
