Hiking backpacks for the Camino: how to choose the right one

Buying a backpack for the Camino or your next hike? Here is my guide to liters, material, comfort and the features that matter most.

Your backpack can quickly become your best non-living friend on a journey. You build a close relationship with it, whether the trip is long or short, so it pays to spend a little time finding the right one. If you are buying a backpack for the Camino or for your next hike, here is my guide to what to look for.

I do not only use my backpacks for long and short hikes. They also come along for cycling, running and outdoor life in general. That is why I keep several different backpacks around, and the first thing you need to decide is how many liters your backpack should hold.

How big should the backpack be?

The size and capacity of a backpack is almost always given in liters. Here is an overview that gives you a good idea of which size suits your needs.

Backpack sizes

SizeLiters and capacityRecommended for
Mini backpacks6 to 10 litersRunning, kids and day trips
Small backpacks10 to 30 litersDay trips, school and work
Medium backpacks30 to 50 litersOvernight trips, hiking and the Camino
Large backpacks50 liters and upThe Camino, round-the-world travel and expeditions

The table is a good starting point, but there are more things to keep in mind before you settle on one.

Best backpack for the Camino

Many people joke on the route about being envious of other pilgrims’ backpacks, and it is not entirely a joke. As close a relationship as you can build with your backpack, that is how annoyed you can also get with it. It is a huge advantage to have chosen the right one before you leave home.

Still, one of the most important things about a backpack is above all how you pack it. Once you have the packing sorted, you get incredibly far, even with a completely ordinary backpack. See my Camino packing list for the best advice.

Things to consider before you buy

So you do not end up with a bad relationship with your backpack, I have gathered the questions worth asking yourself before you buy a hiking backpack.

  • What size should the backpack be?
  • Should it be rain resistant, or should I get a rain cover?
  • What material is the backpack made of?
  • What design do I want?
  • How clever are the features for carrying everything on the packing list?
  • How comfortable is the backpack?
  • Roughly how much can the backpack cost?

Finding the right backpack does not have to be a science. Most modern brands make really good models, and it is often a matter of taste which one you prefer. You get incredibly far by learning to pack properly and perhaps testing or borrowing a couple before you head out on a big trip.

The backpack is not the only thing to think about

Even though heading out to hike does not have to be complicated, the trip is often better when you have prepared the right things at home. You should never cut corners on your gear. There is a lot you cannot control along the way, but you can take care of your equipment.

Some of the most popular categories besides backpacks are hiking boots, trekking poles and sleeping bags. I hope this guide helps you make the decision about which backpack to choose, so you find one that serves you well on many trips to come.

Our recommendations

For the Camino Medium backpack of 30 to 50 liters My favorite for the Camino and overnight trips. Roomy enough for a sleeping bag and a change of clothes without becoming unwieldy.
Small day pack of 10 to 20 liters Light and free on day hikes, runs and bike rides where you only need water, snacks and a jacket.
For long journeys Large backpack of 50 liters and up Great for long journeys and expeditions where you want room for all your outer layers inside the pack itself.
Rain cover as extra insurance Cheap protection against a wet day. It comes with many backpacks, but it is worth checking before you leave.

Frequently asked questions

How many liters should a backpack for the Camino be?
A medium backpack of 30 to 50 liters suits most people on the Camino. If you carry a lot of outer layers or extra gear, a large pack of 50 liters and up gives you more room. For short day trips, 10 to 30 liters is plenty.
What matters most when choosing a backpack?
The single most important thing is actually how you pack it. A backpack that fits your needs and sits well can still feel wrong if the weight is spread unevenly. Learn to pack it properly, and try a few different models before you set off.
Does the backpack need to be waterproof?
Very few backpacks are fully waterproof. Many come with a rain cover, or you can buy one separately. Check the material and consider a rain cover if you will be walking in changeable weather.
What should I consider before I buy?
Look at size, material, rain resistance, comfort, the features that will carry your packing list, the design and the price. Most modern brands make really good backpacks, so a lot of it comes down to personal taste.
What size backpack for day trips?
For running, kids and short day trips, a mini backpack of 6 to 10 liters is plenty. For slightly longer day trips and for school or work, a small backpack of 10 to 30 liters is a good fit.
